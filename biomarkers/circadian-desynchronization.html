Circadian Desynchronization

System ID: BIO-CD
Standard Syntax Verified
The Definition: A serious mismatch between your internal body clock and the real world. This is mostly caused by looking at artificial blue light (screens) at night, which tricks your brain into thinking it is still noon.

The Mechanism

Melatonin Suppression. Your eyes have special sensors that detect daylight. When you look at a phone at night, these sensors signal your brain to stay awake, blocking the sleep hormone you need to rest.

Your Money

It built the Sleep Industrial Complex. Because technology broke our natural sleep, we now spend billions on pills, coffee, energy drinks, and sleep trackers to try and fix the problem we created.

Your Life

It leads to Metabolic Chaos. When your clock is broken, your body doesn't know when to digest food or repair cells. This confusion is a major cause of obesity, diabetes, and depression.

Our World

The Always On Culture. We have effectively erased the night. By trying to make humans productive 24 hours a day, we have violated the basic biological rhythm of the entire planet.

The Experience (System Flaw)

The Glitch is The Midnight Sun. Humans are the only animals that confuse the moon with the sun. We stare into bright lights at midnight and then wonder why we are sick and tired.
