Cortisol Stacking

System ID: BIO-CS
Standard Syntax Verified
The Definition: A condition where stress hormones build up in your body like trash piling up in a room. This happens because modern stress (like emails) does not let you physically run or fight, so the chemicals never leave your blood.

The Mechanism

Open Loop Stress. In the wild, seeing a predator makes you run (using up the stress energy). In an office, stress makes you sit still. The energy has nowhere to go, so it attacks your own body.

Your Money

It powers the Comfort Economy. Because you feel constantly anxious and "wired," you spend money on things to numb the feeling—alcohol, junk food, shopping, and streaming services.

Your Life

The cost is Systemic Inflammation. Having stress hormones "on" all the time ruins your sleep, hurts your heart, weakens your immune system, and literally shrinks the part of your brain used for memory.

Our World

It creates a Reactive Society. When everyone is in survival mode, people lose patience and empathy. They become aggressive and short-tempered because they are biologically looking for a fight.

The Experience (System Flaw)

The Glitch is The False Tiger. Your body's hardware is old; it cannot tell the difference between a lion trying to eat you and a boss sending you a mean email. It reacts to both with the same deadly force.
