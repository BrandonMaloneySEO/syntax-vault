Qualified Immunity

System ID: LEG-QI
Standard Syntax Verified
The Definition: A judicial doctrine that shields government officials (like police officers) from civil lawsuits unless they violated a constitutional right that was already clearly established by a court ruling.

Your Money

The financial cost is absorbed by the plaintiff. The doctrine shields the individual officer from financial responsibility, shifting the burden of damages onto the taxpayer (via government insurance/settlements) or dismissing the case entirely.

Your Life

The biological cost is the institutionalization of impunity. By protecting officials from civil consequence, it structurally guarantees that bad actors remain in positions of power, increasing the risk of violence and rights violations.

Your Time

The doctrine functions as an early dismissal filter. It minimizes the state's temporal cost in court by terminating civil suits at the summary judgment stage before expensive discovery or trial preparation begins.

Our World

The primary output is the Erosion of the Fourth and Fourteenth Amendments. It renders the promise of civil rights and due process unenforceable against state actors, creating a de facto legal caste system.

The Experience (System Flaw)

The paradox is the creation of a No-Win Loop. If the official's conduct has not been ruled on before, the plaintiff loses. If the plaintiff wins, the new ruling establishes the law, but the winning plaintiff receives no damages because the law wasn't clearly established when the harm occurred.
