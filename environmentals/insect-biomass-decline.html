Insect Biomass Decline

System ID: ENV-IBD
Standard Syntax Verified
The Definition: The catastrophic drop in insect populations, often called the Windshield Phenomenon (because you no longer have to clean bugs off your car). We have lost up to 75% of flying insects in some areas in just 30 years.

The Mechanism

Habitat Sterilization. We have paved over wild lands and used powerful pesticides on farms. This removes the flowers insects eat and poisons the plants they live on, leaving them nowhere to go.

Your Money

The Pollination Debt. Insects provide free labor worth billions by pollinating our food. As they die, food prices go up because we have to pay humans or rent bee hives to do the work nature used to do for free.

Your Life

Food Web Collapse. Insects are the foundation of the food chain. They are the food for birds, fish, and frogs. When the bugs die, the larger animals starve, leading to a silent, empty world.

Our World

Ecological Silence. The sound of the world is changing. Forests and fields are becoming quiet as the millions of tiny creatures that "run the world" disappear.

The Experience (System Flaw)

The Glitch is The Invisible Foundation. We obsess over saving big, cute animals like pandas, but we ignore and kill the tiny, ugly creatures that actually keep the biosphere alive.
